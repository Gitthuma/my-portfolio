George Githuma

About

Started out at the National Organisation of Peer Educators (NOPE) in 2006, as a reproductive health trainer of trainer for the Ambassadors of Change (AOC) project and Responding to AIDS among the Youth (RAY). At the same time, I was a member of Youth Peer Education Network-YPEER, an International network of reproductive health trainers. I then preceeded to shortly volunteered, at Population Service International (PSI) after leaving NOPE in 2008. At the organisation, I worked as a youth leader and trainer in primary schools, promoting abstinence as a way to prevent sexual reproductive ilnesses. This was through reproductive health clubs, called "CHILL" (slung for wait) or Abstinence Clubs. In February of the same year, I was invited by Y-PEER, to seat in a committee to help The U.S. President's Emergency Plan for AIDS Relief (PEPFAR), The Warner Bros and private partners including coca cola, to develop a reproductive health HIV&AIDS educational video game, called Pamoja Mtaani. The same year, in October, I was invited by Y-PEER again, to represent African youths at the 30th Anniversary celebrations of The Alma Ata Declaration in Almaty, Kazakhstan. December 2008, I was contracted by Warner Bros to train and manage 36 youth for game research for six months of the pilot phase of the video game testing, followed by nine more months of the implementation phase. In December 2009, I was invited to work with UNICEF New York & Denmark offices, to organise and facilitate a children climate conference named UNICEF Children Climate Forum (CCF), in Copenhagen Denmark. through this convention, "Unite 4 Climate", a child lead advocacy and awareness campaign by UNICEF Zambia, was born. I was invited to consult for this program as a lead trainer. During this time, together with three other collegues, successfully organised and managed three Zambian Children Climate Conference (ZCCC), where more that 600 youth were trained on Climate Change advocacy and awareness; theatre; and multimedia campaigns over a period of six years. 60+ Climate Change campaigns in Zambias ten provinces started as a result. The project is now run by ZCCC I alumnus. June 2010, working for Children's Radio Foundation I Coordinated World Cup In my Village, Zambia. This was the screenng of the first world cup in Africa, in remote villages of Zambia, and making radio shows out of the experience. From this, a network of youth reporters around the country, was born: The name, Youth Reporter Network (YRN), Zambia. I trained these youth to produce radio programs, and use this in community dialogues. One of the radio features produced encouraged UNICEF to fundraise for the construction of a floating school in Mongu, a remote village, west of Zambia. During the same time, I initiated and consulted for a similar program in Tanzania: Young Reporters Network Tanzania. The project has 8 active sites in the country. For more information, please visit my linkedin.

Why Programming

After leaving the NGO world in 2015, I linked up with three interesting guys, who together we formed a collective, and named it Hutchery Studios. The word, comes from the english word, "Hatchery", a place where eggs hatch. Thus, at Hutchery Studios, we use digital audiovisual techniques, to document and present, stories of our time. "We like to think of ourselves as the audiovisual scribes of our time."
